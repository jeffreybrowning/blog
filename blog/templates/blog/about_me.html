A transplant from Missouri, I moved to the Bay Area in 2012. As is typical for many with rather disparate arrays of skillsets--sculpture, law, milieu management, organic gardening, and education--it took some time to find a job that fit. Never being a guy that rested on his laurels however, I messed around with Python to see if I could create some simple games while waiting to hear back from employers. And being the kind of guy whose idea of fun is doing multi-hour depth searches of Wikipedia, I went a little overboard. I got a little obsessed.

While continuing to delve into the depths of introductory programming I took a job with Fusion Academy, a private school catering to students with social, emotional, and academic differences in San Mateo, CA. I spent my first two weeks in the "homework café," where students hung out and did their work. If they needed help on any subject they would ask me, and thus I became a curriculum expert in many subjects. When a math and science teacher quit, I jumped in, read textbooks during my commutes on BART, and took over his classes. Even so, every night I was going home and studying programming.

In February of 2014 I took on an internship with Mightyspring, which gives recruiters tools for interacting with the candidates they manage. . They gave me a programming task—to make human-readable sentences out of relationships between skills, activities, and tooltechs. I did a serviceable job. My code was slow, but it worked. It iterated over the elements too many times. It showed that I could provide a solution, and that I had room for growth.

And here I now sit, still interning at Mighty Spring. I have learned a tremendous amount about paradigms, languages, and frameworks in the past year: functional programming; Matplotlib, SciPy and NumPy for printing graphs; MongoDB and postgreSQL for databases; Django and Flask for making those databases talk to the web. I groked git, which is not a simple task. And at this moment I am also teaching myself front-end development by formatting and designing this blog.
